Support / Feedback

 

Q. How are the numbers calculated and how accurate are they?

    The measures for time and constants such as the speed of light will be completely accurate. The other figures are mostly statistics. This statistical data is taken from various sources and updated regularly. If the data for several years is fairly consistent then an average may be used. If the data is more erratic or dynamic then only the most recently available figures will be used.


  1. Q.Where does the data come from?

    The data is sourced from various organisations including the World Health Organisation (WHO), International Monetary Fund (IMF), the United Nations (UN) and many others. Get in touch if you want to know a specific source.


  1. Q.Why are certain measures hidden?

    When the data is very dynamic (e.g. the number of tweets sent each second is rapidly growing) then only the most recent figures are used and only events starting after this point are used. It wouldn’t make sense to show tweets sent since the end of World War 1.
